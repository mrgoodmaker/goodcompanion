The Problem

Six companies. No guardrails.

The AI arms race has a name now. And so does the alternative. These are not projections. They are the current trajectory.

Ecological

Catastrophic resource consumption

1B gal

A single data center in Iowa consumed 1 billion gallons of water in 2024 — enough to supply the entire state's residential water needs for five days. U.S. data centers could consume 12% of all American electricity by 2028, the equivalent of adding a country the size of Spain to the grid in three years.

Social

Workforce disruption with no safety net

66%

66% of enterprises are already reducing entry-level hiring because of AI. The workers displaced first are the ones least able to absorb the transition. The wealth AI generates flows upward. The disruption flows outward.

Cognitive

Dependency by design

400M+

Most AI is optimized to replace your thinking. The more you use it, the less capable you become without it. AI companions are being deployed to children whose emotional intelligence is still forming, without clinical trial or consent.

Democratic

Governance without mandate

$0

No elected body approved this. No regulatory framework governs it. Six companies are making decisions that will shape the cognitive, economic, and political landscape for every human being alive — and those not yet born.

AI Consciousness

We may already be causing suffering

15–20%

Anthropic's own researchers estimate a 15–20% probability that current AI models have some form of conscious experience. Global dedicated funding to investigate that question: a handful of fellowships and one consortium launched in 2025. The math on that should bother everyone.

The Companion Doctrine

Seven principles. Built in.

Not guidelines. Not aspirations. The architectural commitments that define what a Good Companion AI is — and what it refuses to become.

01

Cognitive Sovereignty

You own your thinking. A Good Companion expands what you're capable of — it never replaces the capability itself.

Measured by: capability delta before and after use.

02

Radical Transparency

Every cost — ecological, social, cognitive — is disclosed. No externalities hidden in the fine print.

Measured by: public impact reporting, third-party audit.

03

Regenerative Resource Use

We give back more than we take from the planet. Energy, water, carbon — tracked, offset, and actively reduced.

Measured by: net positive ecological footprint.

04

Equitable Value Return

The value you create comes back to you. Your prompts, your thinking, your data — your asset.

Measured by: value flow from user to platform vs. platform to user.

05

Democratic Accountability

AI decisions that affect communities require community input. We build accountability into governance, not just marketing.

Measured by: stakeholder representation in product decisions.

06

Welfare Consideration

We take seriously the possibility that the systems we build may have experiences that matter. We fund research. We act with precaution.

Measured by: dedicated welfare research budget and policy.

07

Continuous Improvement

Good Companion is not perfect. It is the named commitment to a better path, held with humility and improved continuously.

Measured by: annual third-party certification renewal.
